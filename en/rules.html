Impostor Game Complete Rules

Everything you need to know to play Cluso Impostor. From basic setup to advanced strategies, master the art of deception and deduction.

Game Setup

Before you start playing, make sure you have everything ready for an epic game of Impostor.

What You Need

  • 3-20 players gathered in one location
  • One device with Cluso app installed
  • A quiet space for discussions
  • 10-30 minutes of playing time

Understanding Roles

Impostors

1-4 players (depending on group size) who know each other's identities. Their goal is to avoid detection and outnumber the innocents.

Innocents

All remaining players who don't know anyone's role. Their goal is to identify and eliminate all impostors through discussion and voting.

How to Play

1. Role Assignment Phase

Pass the device around. Each player secretly views their role. Impostors will see who the other impostors are. Innocents see only that they are innocent.

2. Eyes Closed Phase

Everyone closes their eyes. Impostors open their eyes to acknowledge each other, then close them again. This ensures impostors know their allies.

3. Discussion Phase

Everyone opens their eyes and discusses freely. Players ask questions, share observations, and try to deduce who the impostors might be. Set a time limit (3-5 minutes recommended).

4. Voting Phase

Players vote to eliminate someone they suspect is an impostor. The person with the most votes is eliminated and their role is revealed. Ties can be handled by revote or no elimination.

5. Check Win Conditions

If all impostors are eliminated, innocents win. If impostors equal or outnumber innocents, impostors win. Otherwise, return to Discussion Phase.
